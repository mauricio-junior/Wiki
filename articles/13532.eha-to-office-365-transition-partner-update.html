Transition Update

The EHA-to-Office 365 transition started on May 2012 and will continue for 12-18 months.  We are transitioning customers based their size, tier, and technical constraints.  For information on what to customers can expect during the transition please visit http://GetArchiving.com 

 

Partners interested in knowing their customer’s transition date will need to work with their customer to request for a transition date by opening a EHA support ticket through the EHA Admin Center.

Licensing Update

 

All existing EHA Customers, weather Online and On-Premises transitioning to Office 365 will be provided entitlement to Exchange Online Plan 2.  The implementation of these entitlements will vary based on the type of Volume Licensing agreement. 

The number of entitlements will be determined based on the total number of provisioned users, including active and disabled users, in EHA at the time of the transition.  For more information on provisioned users click here.  

 

Customers with an Existing Enterprise Agreement (including CASA & U.S. Gov’t)

During the transition, customers who have an active Enterprise agreement will be provided with trial licenses for the number of provisioned users to be used for the transition. These trial licenses will be converted to fully paid licenses up-to 30 days post transition for the remainder of your existing agreement.  You will receive an email once the fully paid licenses have been deposited into your tenant.

Customers with a Transitional Microsoft Volume Licensing  or Direct Frontbridge agreements

During the transition, customer who are on or had a Volume Licensing agreement, including Select, Select Plus, Open value, Open Value Subscription or a Direct Forefront agreement and are an active EHA customer, you will be provided with a fully paid license of Exchange Online Plan 2 for a period of 2 years.  Licenses will be provided to customers at the beginning of the transition.

Customer who have purchased EHA but not activated

Customers who have purchased but not activated and are on an Enterprise Agreement will receive entitlement to Exchange Online Plan 2 for the remainder of their existing agreement.  Customer should expect entitlements to the new service over the next 60 days.

EHA Transition Training Session

 

Partners interested in getting up to speed with the advanced archiving features of Office 365 should attend our weekly training session.  Each 60-minute training session is delivered by an archiving specialist and will cover details of the transition including how to perform common archiving tasks in Office 365. 

Training sessions are available during the following times:

•             Mondays @ 8:00 am PT

•             Wednesdays @ 1:00 pm PT

•             Thursdays @ 6:30 am PT

•             Fridays @ 8:00 am PT (beginning August 24)

 

To sign up for a training session, we ask partners to email EHASUP@microsoft.com.
